# ForkRaceFoldTheorems

- Parent README: [../README.md](../README.md)

This subdirectory covers the in-tree constructive theorem package. The shared compiler-emitted kernel theorems referenced by `THM-GNOSIS-*`, including the bounded affine queue-family `continuousHarris` witness package and the bounded coupled-kernel tethering result for inter-app handoff pressure, live in `open-source/gnosis/GnosisProofs.lean` rather than in this subdirectory.

Modules:

- `Claims.lean`: explicit-assumption theorems with constructive proofs for core algebraic/topological identities (including Whip cross-shard crossover properties, weighted queueing expectation balance, finite-prefix truncation balance, the linear-vs-nonlinear fold boundary in §6.12, and the cancellation-target-family impossibility witness for nonlinear folds).
- `MolecularTopology.lean`: constructive molecular-topology theorems, including identical-Betti-signature homological equivalence, the hole-invariance corollary for homology-preserving deformations, and the broader molecular/pipeline topology correspondences.
- `BeautyOptimality.lean`: constructive linear/additive beauty theorems, proving deficit/beauty identities, deficit-monotone latency and waste, strict positive-penalty separation, zero-deficit Pareto optimality for the linear workload model, arbitrary monotone latency/waste objective lifts with strict monotone-objective corollaries, reusable generalized-convex and strict generalized-convex cost signatures, arbitrary deficit-monotone latency/waste profile lifts with monotone, strict generalized-convex, and real-objective corollaries, an indexed comparison-family global-minimum and unique-minimizer theorem for a designated full-fit candidate under strict profiles, abstract failure-Pareto-frontier universal-floor and real-objective theorems under explicit measure-side bounds, an explicit deficit-only boundary witness showing that bare deficit bookkeeping does not order latency/waste, an explicit deficit-dominating failure-tax bridge into that frontier layer, an explicit failure-tax observable bridge into that frontier layer, strict-profile family instances of both bridge layers, additive composition, and an explicit instantiation of the higher-level beauty schema.
- `FailureEntropy.lean`: constructive failure-topology theorems for bounded live-frontier models, including local vent reduction, one-survivor/failure necessity, and coupled-repair debt monotonicity.
- `FailureDurability.lean`: constructive bounded-replica theorems proving quorum preservation under an explicit isolated-failure budget and stable full repair once failures are exhausted.
- `QuorumVisibility.lean`: constructive quorum-intersection and read-after-ack theorems for the bounded protocol layer, plus explicit weak-quorum, contagious-regression, and unfair-repair boundary witnesses.
- `QuorumAsyncNetwork.lean`: constructive connected-quorum availability and committed-read exactness theorems for the bounded async/partition model, plus explicit minority-split and weak-read stale-value boundary witnesses.
- `QuorumConsistency.lean`: constructive committed-read exactness, read-your-writes, and monotonic-read theorems for the bounded session layer, plus pending-read and missing-session-floor boundary witnesses.
- `QuorumOrdering.lean`: constructive multi-writer committed-read exactness, latest-writer tracking, and stale-read-exclusion theorems for globally ordered ballots, plus split-connectivity and ballot-collision boundary witnesses.
- `QuorumLinearizability.lean`: constructive latest-completed-write history-refinement theorems for committed reads, plus the explicit speculative-read boundary witness.
- `FailureFamilies.lean`: constructive failure-topology family theorems separating branch-isolating failure from contagious failure via survivor-projected deterministic fold and repair-debt forcing.
- `FailureTrilemma.lean`: constructive impossibility theorems showing that nontrivial forks cannot collapse to a deterministic single survivor with both zero vent and zero repair debt.
- `FailureComposition.lean`: constructive pipeline theorems lifting the no-free deterministic-collapse boundary across aligned stage sequences and forcing at least one paid stage in any deterministic single-survivor collapse.
- `FailureUniversality.lean`: constructive universality theorems lifting the no-free deterministic-collapse boundary to sparse normalized choice systems and arbitrary-depth recovery trajectories, including the minimum collapse-cost floor `totalVented + totalRepairDebt >= initialLive - 1` and the branch-isolating witness that attains that floor exactly.
- `LandauerBuley.lean`: constructive finite/countable/measurable finite-type thermodynamic calibration theorems showing that equiprobable vented-branch self-information equals frontier Shannon entropy in bits, that the deterministic-collapse failure-tax floor dominates equiprobable erasure, arbitrary finite-support branch-law entropy, and arbitrary Bernoulli binary erasure budgets, that the finite entropy ceiling is attained exactly by the uniform law and the finite failure-tax ceiling exactly by the uniform law on one- or two-branch supports, that arbitrary PMFs already admit both a countable-support entropy shell and a matching countable-support heat shell as `ENNReal` truncation/supremum/counting-measure constructions, that arbitrary source PMFs can be pushed through finite/countable measurable observables into the same coarse-grained entropy/heat shell on the observable codomain, that finitely supported source laws also satisfy deterministic observable entropy/heat monotonicity back to the source law and therefore retain the same frontier/failure-tax/collapse-cost bounds after coarse-graining, that the direct finite-type measurable entropy/heat shell reduces to the finite theorems and lifts their equality characterizations, and that the fair binary fork witness is the exact `kT ln 2` case.
- `SleepDebt.lean`: constructive bounded recovery theorems showing that incomplete recovery leaves positive residual debt and reduced next-cycle capacity, full recovery restores baseline, repeated truncation can preserve or grow carried debt, and debt above threshold enables intrusion-style local venting.
- `SleepDebtSchedule.lean`: constructive coarse repeated-cycle threshold theorems showing that below-threshold and critical schedules stay debt-free while above-threshold schedules accumulate carried debt linearly in cycle count.
- `SleepDebtWeightedSchedule.lean`: constructive weighted repeated-cycle threshold theorems showing that the integerized `20.2 h` literature boundary stays debt-free at and below threshold while supercritical schedules accumulate carried debt linearly in cycle count.
- `FailureController.lean`: constructive controller theorems over the canonical failure-action family, choosing `keep-multiplicity`, `pay-vent`, or `pay-repair` by the minimum weighted coefficient on the exact collapse floor.
- `FailurePareto.lean`: constructive Pareto theorems over the canonical failure-action family, proving that keep, vent, and repair are all non-dominated when the collapse gap is positive.
- `Multiplexing.lean`: constructive turbulent-multiplexing monotonicity theorems for bounded recovered-overlap models.
- `StagedExpansion.lean`: constructive staged-expansion theorems for the bounded diamond witness, comparing shoulder-filling against naive peak widening at equal budget.
- `WarmupEfficiency.lean`: constructive warm-up efficiency theorems for bounded recovered-overlap models, deriving the exact threshold where weighted Wallace reduction beats added Buley cost.
- `WarmupController.lean`: constructive controller theorems for the burden/redline policy that chooses `expand`, `constrain`, or `shed-load` under one-hot topology mismatch.
- `Wallace.lean`: constructive Wallace/crank theorems for bounded three-layer frontiers, including complement, boundedness, and the symmetric diamond closed form.
- `MeasureQueueing.lean`: constructive infinite-support queueing theorems over `tsum`, countably supported stochastic laws via `PMF`, and `lintegral`, including monotone truncation-to-limit conservation.
- `QueueBoundary.lean`: constructive one-path queue boundary witness that packages the canonical stable `M/M/1` case with `β₁ = 0`, capacity `1`, and stationary mean occupancy `λ / (μ - λ)`.
- `QueueStability.lean`: constructive queue-family theorems for the stable `M/M/1` stationary occupancy law plus trajectory-level Cesaro/ergodic balance for unbounded open-network sample paths.
- `JacksonQueueing.lean`: constructive finite-node Jackson-network product-form occupancy theorems plus witness constructors from the raw Jackson data. The explicit `α` recipe is `α_spec = λ (I - P)^{-1}` under `spectralRadius P < 1`, prove the traffic equations and nodewise bounds `α_spec >= 0`, `α_spec < μ`, instantiate `spectralNetworkData`, and then use the Knaster-Tarski-style dominance bridge to transfer those bounds to `constructiveNetworkDataOfSpectral`. The file now also exposes a raw-data sufficient criterion organized as a finite-step envelope ladder `throughputEnvelopeApprox n`, starting from the `maxExternalArrival`/`maxIncomingRoutingMass` envelope at `n = 0`, recovering `localThroughputEnvelope` at `n = 1`, recovering `secondOrderThroughputEnvelope` at `n = 2`, proving the ladder is descending, adding scalar residual/error certificates around `α_spec`, and adding a routing-shaped node-local residual ladder `throughputResidualApprox n`; direct nodewise service bounds against any step of that ladder remain available, and the `minServiceRate` corollary still packages the coarsest automatic route to the corresponding constructive/spectral network data and measure entry points. It also mechanizes adaptive comparison: `AdaptiveJacksonTrafficData`, domination by an explicit ceiling kernel, and the `supremumKernel` specialization when the pointwise ceiling remains substochastic.
- `JacksonExactClosure.lean`: constructive exact Jackson fixed-point closure that packages uniqueness of the real traffic solution under `spectralRadius P < 1`, promotes any nonnegative stable real fixed point into direct constructive network witnesses, and closes the same mean-occupancy and `lintegral` balance laws without passing through the envelope ladder; the spectral/resolvent route is the canonical corollary.

- `StateDependentQueueFamilies.lean`: concrete state-dependent queue-family wrappers for vacation, retrial, reneging, and adaptive-routing kernels, together with balance theorems routed through the shared stability schema and a raw-parameter two-node adaptive rerouting witness that derives its own ceiling kernel, spectral side conditions, throughput bound, and linear drift witness.
