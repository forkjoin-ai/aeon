Title: Being Irreversible: A Theory of Directed Process Under Conservation and Ground State

Author: Taylor William Buley

Abstract:
This paper studies a family of irreversible computations organized around the
weighting rule w_i = R - min(v_i, R) + 1. Within the modeled choice systems in
the paper, the +1 term is structural rather than parametric: it prevents any
weight from reaching zero and preserves a nonzero residual mass over the
remaining options. Removing the term yields a deterministic eliminator;
retaining it yields a bounded learning rule with an explicit floor, an
observation-dependent gain, and falsifiable downstream consequences.
The formal surface currently includes 3,158+ machine-checked theorems across
195 Lean 4 modules (zero sorry). The companion corpus organizes a reduction
chain from theorem-level statements to seven laws, three axioms, one formula,
and the successor-floor interpretation of the +1 term, and it records
thirty-five falsifiable predictions across the modeled domains.
The framework is instantiated in nine executable engineering layers -- formal
verification, programming language, scheduling, transport protocol,
compression, mixture-of-agents routing, language-model inference,
protocol-as-execution, and void walking -- built from four primitives (fork,
race, fold, vent) under conservation, irreversibility, and termination
constraints. Each layer is implemented, tested, and benchmarked: chunked
pipelining yields 3.1x-267x modeled speedup under explicit idealizations,
codec racing selects the best codec among the evaluated set on a per-chunk
basis, and Vickrey Table inference yields 4.8x measured throughput.
