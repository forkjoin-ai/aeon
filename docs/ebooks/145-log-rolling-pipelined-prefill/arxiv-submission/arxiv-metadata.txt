Title: Being Irreversible: A Theory of Directed Process Under Conservation and Ground State

Author: Taylor William Buley

Abstract:
Every irreversible transformation creates structure in the space of what was
not chosen. This paper identifies the structure, proves its properties, and
shows it is the same structure at every scale where irreversibility operates.
We decompose directed irreversible process into four primitives -- fork (create
parallel paths), race (select among them), fold (merge results irreversibly),
vent (dissipate what the fold cannot preserve) -- and three constraints --
conservation (nothing is created or destroyed), irreversibility (folds generate
Landauer heat), ground state (every trajectory terminates). Within this
framework, failure is not the opposite of success. It is the sufficient
statistic from which success is derived. Every fold vents N - 1 paths for each
path it keeps. The rejected paths accumulate in a void boundary whose
complement distribution is a deterministic, monotone, convergent probability
measure over the surviving options. The framework yields five results:
(1) a computational topology where the Betti deficit measures deviation from
efficient structure; (2) a probability theory (Buleyean probability) derived
entirely from what things are not, subsuming Bayesian and Solomonoff inference;
(3) a thermodynamics linking topological deficit to Landauer heat; (4) a theory
of communication and peace where shared context monotonically deflates semiotic
deficit; (5) immanent self-hosting where the framework certifies itself from
inside with no external oracle. The framework is instantiated across nine
domains with 500+ Lean 4 theorems (zero sorry), 90+ TLA+ specifications, and
200+ executable companion tests.
