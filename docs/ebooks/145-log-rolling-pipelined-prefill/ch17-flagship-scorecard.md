# Chapter 17 Flagship Scorecard

- Parent volume README: [README.md](./README.md)
- Full theory chapter: [ch17-arxiv-manuscript.md](./ch17-arxiv-manuscript.md)
- Flagship reviewer-facing manuscript: [ch17-arxiv-manuscript-flagship.md](./ch17-arxiv-manuscript-flagship.md)

This scorecard is comparative rather than promotional. It tracks what the flagship draft is trying to improve relative to the full theory chapter.

| Dimension | Full theory chapter | Flagship manuscript |
|---|---|---|
| Thesis sharpness | Multiple contribution families compete for the center of gravity. | One claim: topological mismatch in distributed inference. |
| Claim discipline | Mixes bounded systems claims with broader correspondence material. | Keeps only finite-DAG scheduling, bounded protocol claims, and artifact-backed evaluation. |
| Evidence locality | Strong local evidence exists, but it is surrounded by distant analogies. | Every main-body numeric claim is tied to gate1, gate2, or a named theorem family. |
| Reviewer legibility | Ambitious but difficult to summarize in one sentence. | Abstract, opening thesis, and conclusion all point at the same bridge claim. |
| Section economy | Large sweep with many adjacent programs and side arguments. | Narrow section order: model, proofs, instantiation, measurement. |
| Artifact traceability | Deep companion surface, but too much of it is in play at once. | Small theorem-to-artifact map with only the artifacts the flagship body names. |
| Limitation honesty | Limitations exist but compete with expansionary framing. | Non-claims are explicit and local to the distributed-systems scope. |
| Submission readiness | Broad theory chapter, harder to defend against a hostile reviewer. | Reviewer-facing draft with bounded thesis and separate packaging path. |
