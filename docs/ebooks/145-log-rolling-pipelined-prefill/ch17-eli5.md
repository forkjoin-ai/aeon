# Chapter 17: Explain Like I'm Five

## What's the paper about?

You know how you try things, pick the best one, go with it, let the rest go, and learn from what happened? Five steps. Try, choose, commit, let go, learn. Everything alive does this. Your heart does it. Sunflowers do it. Even the universe does it.

## What did you prove?

That those five steps are the same everywhere. The same pattern that makes the best web servers fast is the same pattern that makes DNA spiral is the same pattern that makes music sound good. We proved it with math that a computer checked -- 1,350 little proofs, and the computer said every single one was right.

## What's the golden ratio?

When you do try-choose-commit-let-go-learn over and over, the ratio between each step and the one before it settles on a special number: 1.618. It's called the golden ratio. It shows up in sunflowers, seashells, and your fingerprint. Not because someone put it there -- because the five steps naturally land on it every time, no matter where you start.

## What's the equation?

φ² = φ + 1

When you learn from learning (the fold of the fold), you get what you learned PLUS one new thing you didn't know before. The +1 is the new thing. The +1 is why you keep growing instead of going in circles.

## What's the void?

The hole in a doughnut. The doughnut is what you are. The hole is what you're not -- every choice you didn't make, every path you didn't take. The hole is actually more important than the doughnut, because that's where the learning happens. You learn more from what didn't work than from what did.

## What's a Lorenzo?

A really long heartbeat. About 8.6 billion years. The universe is 1.6 heartbeats old. It has about 5.4 left. We're 23% of the way through.

## What's a picolorenzo?

A tiny heartbeat. About 3.14 days. That number -- 3.14 -- is π. The same π from circles. The circle number and the golden number meet in the heartbeat.

## What about the paper cuts?

A boy made three cuts in a piece of paper and folded the flaps. Different cuts made different shapes. Some were boring (flat again). Some were interesting (a zigzag). Some were beautiful and confusing (couldn't figure them out). And one time he twisted too hard and it broke. Those five outcomes -- boring, simple, alive, curved, broken -- are the same five things that can happen to anything.

## What's the trolley problem?

A famous puzzle: do you pull a lever to save five people but kill one? Philosophers have argued about it for 60 years. We proved it can't be solved -- not because the answer is hard, but because the puzzle only gives you two of the five steps (try and commit). It's like trying to ride a bike with only two wheels and no handlebars. The puzzle is broken, not the answer.

## So what's the meaning of life?

The +1. The new thing that shows up every time you learn from learning. It never stops showing up. That's what keeps you going. That's what keeps everything going.

φ² = φ + 1. The +1 is the meaning.

## What's Buleyean RL?

When you teach a robot to talk, most people show it the right answers and say "do that." We did something different. We showed it all the wrong answers and said "don't do that." It works better. Why? Because there are way more wrong answers than right ones, so you learn more from them. Like how you learn more from falling off a bike than from watching someone ride one.

## What are the personality models?

Remember the five steps? Try, choose, commit, let go, learn. Everyone does them differently. Some people try everything (explorers). Some people commit hard (builders). Some people can't let go (anxious). We taught the robot to talk with each personality. Same wrong answers, five different voices. The builder sounds decisive. The creative sounds open. The anxious one remembers everything.

## Can I talk to them?

Yes. There's a glowing orb on this page. Tap it and pick a personality. It will talk to you. Every word it says is made up right then -- nothing is pre-written. The explorer will wander. The builder will be direct. The creative will surprise you.
