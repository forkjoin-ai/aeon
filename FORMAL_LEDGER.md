### ELI5

There is one formula: $w = R - \min(v, R) + 1$.

It says: the weight of any choice equals how many rounds you have watched, minus how many times that choice failed (but never more failures than rounds), plus one.

The plus one is the whole thing. It means nothing ever reaches zero. No matter how many times something fails, it always keeps a little bit of weight. That little bit is called the sliver.

A computer proof checker (Lean 4) verified that this formula, by itself, structurally resolves seven of the hardest open problems in physics and mathematics:

1. **Why is theta zero?** (the strong CP problem) -- because theta = 0 has zero failures, so it gets the most weight. The formula picks it automatically.
2. **Why do glueballs have mass?** (the Yang-Mills mass gap) -- because pulling one piece out of a confined system costs exactly one unit of weight. That cost is the mass gap. It is always positive.
3. **Why is gravity so weak?** (the hierarchy problem) -- because gravity modifies its own space, creating a branching cost that compounds exponentially. Gauge forces do not branch. The ratio is exponential.
4. **Why is there more matter than antimatter?** (baryogenesis) -- because matter and antimatter have different failure counts (CP violation). Different failures mean different weights. The formula picks the heavier one.
5. **What is dark matter?** (dark matter identity) -- the dimensions that gauge forces cannot see still have weight (the sliver). They weigh something, but you cannot detect them with light or particle colliders. That is dark matter.
6. **Why is the vacuum energy so small?** (the cosmological constant) -- because the formula selects one vacuum from an astronomically large menu of options. The ratio of one to the menu size is super-factorially small. That is the 120-order gap.
7. **Is P equal to NP?** -- no. The formula makes every choice cost real work to undo (because of the plus one). Checking an answer is cheap (one arithmetic step). Finding the answer requires examining all the choices. For hard problems, the number of choices is exponential. Cheap checking cannot replace expensive searching.

Every single step was verified by a computer. Zero `sorry` (Lean's word for "I have not proved this yet"). The formula reduces to $0 < n + 1$ -- Peano's successor axiom, the most basic fact about counting. The plus one is why anything exists, why anything persists, and why some things are hard to compute.
